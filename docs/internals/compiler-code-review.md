# Code-Quality & Architecture Review — The VL Self-Hosted Compiler (`compiler/`)

> Generated by an independent review agent (2026-06-27), analyzing `compiler/*.vl` purely from
> first principles of compiler construction and software engineering — no task context. Captured here
> for follow-up; not yet acted on. Line numbers reflect the state at time of review and will drift.

## Executive summary

This is a genuinely impressive, mature self-hosted compiler: ~51k lines of VL implementing a full
lex → parse → typecheck → WASM-GC emit pipeline, with a clean discriminated-union arena AST (`ast.vl`),
a faithful hand-written lexer (`lexer.vl`), a precedence-climbing parser (`parser.vl`), and a
four-way-split emit support layer (`emit_base`/`emit_bytes`/`emit_query`/`emit_state`) that is acyclic
and well-documented. The engineering discipline is high — errors are accumulated as values (no
exceptions), bounds-checks are pervasive and deliberate, and the comments are extraordinary, frequently
documenting *why* a workaround exists. The dominant architectural weakness is a single through-line:
**type identity is represented as synthetic strings and integer "kind" codes that are built in the
parser, re-parsed by hand-rolled scanners in the typechecker, and string-/integer-tested again in the
emitter** — a stringly-typed, magic-number representation that drives most of the duplication,
fragility, and the two 1,000+-line dispatcher functions. Critically, the typechecker's soundness
verdicts are entangled with what the *emitter* can currently lower, conflating "is this well-typed?"
with "can we codegen this?". Overall assessment: **architecturally sound skeleton, strong craftsmanship,
but carrying heavy structural debt concentrated in the type representation and two mega-files
(`wasmEmit.vl` at 27.8k lines, `typecheck.vl` at 8.7k).** Many of the worst smells are honestly
documented as forced by the VL host's current limitations (no map-typed struct fields, no
self-referential union aliases, no closures) — flagged throughout so they aren't mistaken for
carelessness.

## Prioritized findings

### CRITICAL

**C1. A stringly-typed type representation threaded across all three phases.**
Type identity is carried as synthetic name strings — `"i32[]"`, `"(i32|null)"`, `"A&B"`, `"!A"`,
`"{[string]:V}"`, `"(i32)=>i32"`, `"a"|"b"`. The lifecycle spans the whole pipeline:
- **Built in the parser** by string concatenation: `parseTypeName`, `parseTypeIsect`, `parseTypeAtom`
  join members with `"|"`, `"&"`, `"!"`, `"[]"`.
- **Re-parsed in the checker** by a 270-line ad-hoc recursive-descent scanner over strings, `nameToTy`
  (`typecheck.vl:3084-3355`), self-recursing 28×, with its own paren-depth counters,
  `topLevelArrowIndex`, and `"]:"`-scanning for maps. The checker has **56** `.slice`/split sites on
  type strings, and decides type *equality* by rendering both sides to strings and `==`-comparing them
  (`tyToStr(initTy) != tyToStr(annot)`, `typecheck.vl:2127`).
- **String-tested again in the emitter** — **68** occurrences of `== "i32"` / `== "f64"` / `"|null"` /
  `"[]"` substring tests.

The grammar of these names is encoded implicitly in five+ places (`nameToTy`, `tyToStrGo`,
`tyToEmitName`, `canonEmitName`, the `split*` helpers), which must all agree on paren-wrapping, `|null`
vs `?`, and separators — and currently *don't*: `tyToStr` renders `") -> "`, `tyToEmitName` renders
`") => "`, `canonEmitName` expects `=>`.
**Recommendation:** Consolidate parsing/rendering into **one** `parseTypeName`/`renderTypeName` pair with
a single grammar that every site calls. Immediate win: unify `tyToStr` and `tyToEmitName` (differ only
in separators) — removes ~100 lines and a class of "the two renderers disagreed" bugs. Longer term,
thread the structured `Ty` arena (already exists, `typecheck.vl:97-148`) to the emitter instead of
re-deriving from strings.

**C2. Uncentralized integer "kind" codes — a decentralized magic-number enum.**
The emitter discriminates every value by an integer kind code (0=i32, 1=struct, 2=i32[], 3=string,
4=union-box, 5=ref-list, … 21=f32[]), but **there is no central enum or constant table.** The canonical
mapping lives only inside `vtKindOfType` (`wasmEmit.vl:16893-16929`). These raw integers are compared as
bare literals **1,066 times** across `wasmEmit.vl`. Worse, the numbering is **not consistent across
subsystems**: `mfRecvKindOf` uses 1=f64-list/2=ref-list/3=string-list, while `vtKindOfType` uses
12=f64[]/5=ref-list/7=string[], and `stmtIsPushKind` uses a third scheme. The `$fnsig` ABI compounds
this: `cloParamTok`, `fbValtype`, and `annParamKind` are a hand-synchronized triple keyed off these same
uncentralized codes.
**Recommendation:** Define one canonical set of named constants (`KIND_I32 = 0`, …) with a single
numbering, and replace the bare literals. Single highest-leverage change in the file.

**C3. Two 1,000+-line dispatcher functions.**
`checkNodeReal` (`typecheck.vl:6659-7941`, ~1,283 lines, **95** `is <Variant>` arms, ~5 levels deep) and
`emitExpr` (`wasmEmit.vl:9565-10902`, ~1,338 lines). Close behind: `emitModule` (944), `emitStmt` (764),
`emitCall` (`wasmEmit.vl:11854`, 644), `checkCallNode` (`typecheck.vl:4776`, 632), `checkFuncDeclNode`
(538).
**Recommendation:** Split each dispatcher into per-category helpers dispatched from a thin jump table.
Extract the builtin-method / member-call intrinsic ladders into table-driven helpers.

### HIGH

**H1. The typechecker's soundness is entangled with emitter capability.**
"emitter / lower / wasm / box / fnsig" appears **162 times** in `typecheck.vl`. Several genuine *type
errors* are actually admissions that the emitter can't lower a construct yet — e.g. "cannot infer a
union return type … annotate" (`typecheck.vl:6073-6124`, `8064-8157`), gated on predicates like
`valueUnionRetName(inferred) == ""`. The checker therefore **rejects well-typed programs because of
downstream codegen gaps**, and if those predicates drift from the emitter it will over- or under-reject.
~13 exported `node*` queries (`typecheck.vl:8236-8401`) exist only to feed emitter metadata back out.
**Recommendation:** Quarantine emitter-metadata into a separate section, and route "unsupported
lowering" through a *distinct diagnostic channel* so the type system's judgment is never conflated with
codegen maturity.

**H2. Per-type / per-list-kind copy-paste ladders (the dominant DRY violation).**
The "fan one operation across every element/scalar type" pattern recurs everywhere:
- The `exprXArray` family — `exprArray`/`exprRefArray`/`exprStringArray`/`exprF64Array`/`exprI64Array`/
  `exprF32Array` — six parallel walks differing only in element type.
- The list-kind classifier ladder (`isRef/isStr/isF64/isI64/isF32` then a 5-way dispatch) recurs ~6×
  (`stmtIsPushKind`, `callIsMemberPopKind`, the reservation walkers).
- The push/pop scratch-reservation AST walkers (`blockHasPushKind`/`ifChainHasPushKind`,
  `blockHasMemberPopKind`, `collectRefPushSlots`) are 3+ copies of the same Block/If/While/For descent.
- The `fRet*` / `ret*Flag` return-kind flag families: ~14–16 parallel one-line wrappers
  (`emit_base.vl:949-1049`; `cloRetKindOf` probes ten tables in series).
- In the checker, the 8-way scalar set (`i32|i64|f32|f64|boolean|string|i32[]|string[]`) is open-coded
  as `||` chains in `recordRedundantRet`, `isClassifiableRetName`, `isNumeric`, etc.
**Recommendation:** Drive these from a single **kind-descriptor table** (one row per kind: backing type,
wrapper, predicate, scratch-frame id) and write the traversal/dispatch *once*. Make the AST-walk
skeletons one generic walker parameterized by a leaf predicate.

**H3. Duplicated IEEE float encoders and triple kind→heaptype ladders in the support layer.**
`f64Bytes` (`emit_base.vl:73-205`) and `f32Bytes` (`:206-~330`) are two ~130-line decimal→IEEE encoders
with identical structure, differing only in significand width / exponent bias / output width — the most
error-prone duplication since the rounding code is subtle and maintained in two copies. Separately,
`fbValtype`/`fbValtypeNullable`/`fbRefNullForKind` (`emit_bytes.vl:873-1082`) are three parallel
kind→heap-index ladders plus a triplicated out-of-bounds guard; every new rep kind must be added in
three places.
**Recommendation:** Parameterize one `ieeeBytes(text, mantBits, bias, nbytes)`. Factor
`heapIdxOfKind(kind, structIdx)` so the three valtype functions become thin wrappers.

### MEDIUM

**M1. Type equality decided by string rendering; `tyToStr` a self-cited hotspot.** The checker compares
types by rendering to strings and `==`-comparing (`typecheck.vl:2127`, `2231`, `6231`) — O(type-size)
render + string compare where structural equality would do. The code cites `tyToStr` as a "#494 ~4×
self-compile cost" hotspot (`:8375-8388`); **114** `tyToStr(` call sites.
**Recommendation:** Add a structural `tyEq(a, b)` over the `Ty` arena; reserve `tyToStr` for diagnostics.

**M2. Linear-scan lookups on hot paths.** Many per-function/per-shape lookups remain linear:
`localIndexOf`/`paramIndexOf`/`declaredIndexOf`, `sFieldIndex`, `rlSlotByName`, `structIndexByName` (all
by name string-compare, per node). `symOccHasTok` (`typecheck.vl:2020`) does a full linear scan on
**every** declaration/use — O(occurrences²). `nliAtomOfExpr`/`letInfStrListByUse` scan the entire node
arena despite `fnNames` existing.
**Recommendation:** Back by-name tables with maps; replace `symOccHasTok` with a token-keyed set; cache
`vtKindOfType` per annotation node.

**M3. The `-1` sentinel is overloaded** — simultaneously "no type", "unknown name", "empty-collection
hole", and "scope-not-found" (`typecheck.vl:3212-3216` documents a real past crash). `TY_ERR`/`TyVar`
are assignable-to-anything *and* numeric-permissive, so a genuine error mid-expression silently
satisfies downstream checks.
**Recommendation:** Distinct sentinels (a `TyHole` variant separate from `TyErr`/`TyVar`).

**M4. Module-global state as hand-managed parallel-array stacks.** The checker has **74** mutable module
globals, many parallel-array stacks (`pendNames`/`pendTys`/`pendAts`/`pendDepths`, …).
`monoInferListElem` manually snapshots and truncates **14 separate arrays**. The byte layer expresses
the scratch-frame layout *three times* (`fbBeginFunc`/`fbLocalsCount`/`fbEmitLocalsVec`).
**Recommendation:** Largely host-forced (no map-typed struct fields), honestly documented — group stacks
into structs where possible; add a debug assertion that `fbLocalsCount` matches `fbEmitLocalsVec`.

**M5. wasm opcodes as commented magic numbers** — `fbI32Bin(107) // 0x6b i32.sub`,
`wU8(212) // 0xd4 ref.as_non_null` (194 `fbI32Bin`, 292 raw `wU8` in wasmEmit; 133 in emit_bytes).
**Recommendation:** Named opcode constants (`I32_SUB = 107`, `GC_PREFIX = 251`) or builder methods.

### LOW

- **L1.** Token re-scanning for fix spans in the checker (`recordRedundantAnnot`/`recordRedundantRet`,
  `typecheck.vl:2116-2262`) — parser-level work living in the checker.
- **L2.** Duplicated "cannot infer …" diagnostic wording, 6+ near-identical sites.
- **L3.** Dead assert-oracle in the hot path (`mAssignTypeIndices`, `wasmEmit.vl:18231`) — "scheduled
  for deletion one release after Phase G".
- **L4.** Comment-to-code ratio: many functions have comments longer than their bodies (load-bearing
  *because* of the magic-number opacity — a symptom of C1/C2).
- **L5.** Kind legend half-documented (`fbValtype` header documents kinds 0–8; body handles 0–21).

## Genuine strengths

1. **Sound AST design** — discriminated-union-by-shape arena AST; kind is the runtime variant; distinct
   field-name prefixes keep `is`-discrimination unambiguous; arena-index children sidestep VL's lack of
   self-referential union aliases.
2. **Disciplined, exception-free error handling** — errors are values; every lookup bounds-checked with
   a "never a trap" contract; parser always makes progress and recovers via `ErrExpr`.
3. **Clean, acyclic emit support layering** — `emit_bignum` → `emit_base` → `emit_query` (pure) and
   `emit_state` → `emit_bytes` (byte-writers).
4. **Faithful, idiomatic lexer and precedence-climbing parser** — incl. the `pendingGt` generic-close
   `>>` un-fusing.
5. **Outstanding documentation** — nearly every non-obvious decision, host workaround, and invariant is
   explained, including the past bugs that motivated them.
6. **Real soundness analyses** — definite-assignment, missing-return, and cyclic-arena recursion guards.

**Bottom line:** The root cause of nearly every High/Medium finding is one disease — type identity
represented as uncentralized integer kind-codes (C2) and re-parsed synthetic strings (C1) rather than a
structured value threaded through the phases. Highest-ROI program of work: (1) centralize the kind codes
as named constants with one numbering; (2) consolidate type-name parsing/rendering into one grammar and
add structural `tyEq`; (3) split the two 1,000-line dispatchers; (4) decouple the checker's soundness
verdicts from emitter capability.
